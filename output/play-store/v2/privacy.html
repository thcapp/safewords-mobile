Privacy policy

Safewords

Last updated: 28 April 2026

Safewords is a tool that lets a family or trusted group share a rotating "safeword" they can ask each other for during phone calls — to defend against AI voice-cloning scams. The app is designed to work entirely on your device.

What we collect

Nothing. Safewords does not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal information. There is no account, no login, no analytics, no advertising, and no telemetry.

What stays on your device

  • Your group seeds (used to derive the rotating safeword)
  • Group names and the names you give your circle
  • App preferences (rotation interval, biometric lock, accessibility mode, etc.)
  • Optional emergency override word

Seeds and override words are stored using Android's EncryptedSharedPreferences (hardware-backed where available). Deleting the app removes everything.

What we send over the network

Nothing. Safewords makes no network requests. The only time data leaves your device is when you deliberately share it — for example, by displaying a QR code so a family member can scan it, or by sending an SMS invite using your phone's normal messaging app.

Permissions

  • Camera — used only when you tap "Scan QR" to join a group. The camera is never used in the background.
  • Biometric — used only if you turn on "Require biometrics to open" in Settings.
  • SMS (optional, indirect) — when you tap "Invite via SMS", we open your default SMS app with a prefilled invite. We never send messages on your behalf.

Children

Safewords is suitable for all ages. Because we collect no personal data, there is nothing to disclose under COPPA, GDPR-K, or similar regimes.

Third parties

None. Safewords does not embed third-party SDKs for analytics, crash reporting, advertising, or any other purpose.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the "last updated" date above will change. We will not retroactively expand data collection without an explicit, opt-in update.
